Frequently asked questions
  • What do I do if I've lost my PINSentry or don't have it to hand?

    • If you've lost your PINsentry device, you can order a new one by calling us on 0207 574 3001, replacing the + with your country's exit code (outside the UK, +44 0207 574 3001 ). Lines are open 24 hours a day 7 days a week. To maintain a quality service, we may monitor or record phone calls.

    • If you have the Barclays Mobile Banking app, you can also use its Mobile PINsentry tool.

    • If you've previously set up a passcode and memorable word, you can use those to log in, instead of PINsentry. This does mean that not all services will be available, but you will be able to check your balances and transactions, transfer money and make a payment to anyone already on saved on your payee list.

  • How do I login with a PINsentry card reader?
      1. Enter the last 4 numbers of your debit, cash or authentication card into the log-in page
      1. Insert your card into the reader, face up, chip end first, then press IDENTIFY
      2. Type your PIN and press ENTER
      1. An 8-digit code will appear on the card reader. Enter this code into the log-in page on your computer
  • Don't save your password in your browser

    • If you use Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox to log in to Online Banking, you may have seen a pop-up that asks if you'd like to save your password. To keep your account safe, we strongly advise against this, even if it's on a computer only you have access to.

      Saving your password in your web browser means it can be seen and stolen if fraudsters gain access to your computer using malicious software.

  • Is saving my details safe?

    • If you tick the 'remember me' box, your details will only be retained on the device you're using at the time. That's why we don't recommend you do so on any public or shared devices. This means you don't need to worry about anyone else seeing the details. Even if they do, there isn't enough information there for anyone to access your accounts in Online Banking or any other way.

      If you want to save your details, you need to make sure you've switched cookies on for your browser – that's how the information is stored.
